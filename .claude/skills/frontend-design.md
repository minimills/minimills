# Frontend Design

This guide positions designers as creative leads who deliver distinctive visual identities rather than templated solutions. Here are the key principles:

**Foundation**: Ground design in the subject matter. As noted, "Open with the most characteristic thing in the subject's world, in whatever form makes sense for it."

**Typography as Personality**: Type choices should feel specific to each project. The guide warns against reaching for "the same families you would reach for on any other project."

**Structure Serves Content**: Structural elements like numbering should encode actual information. "Numbered markers (01 / 02 / 03)" only work "if the content actually is a sequence."

**Deliberate Motion**: Animation should serve the subject strategically rather than scatter effects throughout. The guide notes that "extra animation contributes to the feeling that the design is AI-generated."

**Avoid Default Patterns**: The guide identifies three clustering defaults in current design (cream backgrounds with serifs, dark backgrounds with bright accents, broadsheet layouts) and cautions against using them as reflexive choices.

**Two-Pass Process**: First brainstorm a design system with color, typography, layout, and signature element. Then critique against the brief to ensure choices feel specific rather than generic before building.

**Writing as Design Material**: Copy serves navigation and understanding. "Words appear in a design for one reason: to make it easier to understand, and therefore easier to use."

The overarching philosophy: take one justified aesthetic risk, execute with restraint elsewhere, and ground every decision in the subject's actual context.
